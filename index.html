About Me

Let’s pickup my story from my junior year of College. I was working on my Bachelor's in Finance at Pace University, NYC and landed a position with Morgan Stanley's Equity Derivatives Group. My responsibilities included overseeing processes that calculated options valuation, profit/loss, and portfolio risk; then reporting those results. Morgan Stanley had a different proprietary program to perform each of those tasks. The underlying data and calculations were a mystery to me, but everything on the reporting end came from Excel spreadsheets that I managed and it was there that I got my first taste of programming.

I worked with a brilliant junior analyst who had built some nifty functionalities into Excel with VBA. Once he showed me that Developer tab I was hooked. We were soon moved to different teams but I would constantly pester him with questions and eventually started tinkering with the VBA code.

I graduated in 2008, and in that year Morgan Stanley went thru the biggest crisis they ever faced. They were unable to give me a competitive offer and it did not take long for me to find a very attractive position at a hedge fund in Stamford, CT. Before leaving Morgan Stanley I had the pleasure of meeting the Director in charge of their automated options trading desk (someone who would always receive my reports but that I never actually met in person). What was supposed to be a 10 minute goodbye turned into an hour long conversation once I saw his computer screen and asked what were those cryptic sentence fragments all about. That was the first time I laid my eye on “real” computer code (probably python). Side note: that day he also told me that all you need to know to do his job [besides coding] is in this book.

Diamondback Capital was multi-billion dollar multi strategy long/short hedge fund. If you had all your money invested with them in 2008-2009 you would not have been disappointed with your returns. While banks were shutting down, they were hiring across the board. My position there was very similar to my analyst position at MS: collect data, reconcile data, report data. But at Diamondback my team did not have the luxury of dedicated programmers handling data collection and maintenance with sophisticated databases and proprietary systems. It was my responsibility to manage all the data day to day using mostly Excel (auditors would make sure we weren’t misreporting anything once a month, the dreaded month-end process).

It was there that I took my VBA skills to another level, mostly out of an insatiable desire to make every process more efficient. After a few months, I was able to reduce the time it took to reconcile the previous day’s P&L from 4 hours to 2. My manager noticed the improvement when he saw me having lunch at 11am one day, and he promptly gave me more responsibilities to fill the idle time. After 1 year working there, I was training someone to take over my day to day responsibilities and moving on to work on bigger projects within the firm. Diamondback eventually shut down, after that I held positions in different firms. But the story was always the same, I would leave things better than I found them.

Then in 2015 I was offered the position of Director of Operations for a hedge fund in Florida. That is the highest position in the Operations department of hedge funds. By that point I was a bit removed from the day to day processes. My goal was still to improve them but now it mostly involved things like hiring the right people and looking into different systems. I was no longer coding daily (I would occasionally teach VBA to junior colleagues).

I realized then that the most satisfying part of the job for me was being faced with a difficult problem, coming up with an elegant solution for it, and then getting my hands dirty implementing it. Around that time I started looking into Computer Science programs but could not find one that would fit into my busy schedule.

Then, like all good things, the Director position came to an abrupt end after I had a major disagreement with the other managers at the fund. I left Florida and lived in Hawaii for two years, where I spent my time surfing, reading, taking free online courses and pondering about the next stage of my life…

I am not even sure how I came across the online post-bacc Computer Science program from Oregon State, but it took me exactly 24 hours from the time I first read the curriculum to when I made the decision to embark on this journey.
